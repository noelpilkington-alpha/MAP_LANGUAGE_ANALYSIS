4. MobyMax-Only Students: A Nuanced Picture

Counterintuitive Finding

For MobyMax-only students, those who mastered 0 grades show higher growth (2.47x) than those who mastered 1-2 grades. Possible explanations:

  • Active engagement: Students still working through content may be more engaged with the material
  • Pacing: Students who quickly mastered grades may have moved too fast without deep learning
  • Ceiling effect: High performers who mastered quickly may have less room to grow

Answers to Head of Academics' Questions

Question Answer
Writing/AlphaWrite: Does mastery matter? YES, mastery matters (+1.33x). (CORRECTED using passed test data) Students who passed Writing tests show 3.16x growth vs 1.83x for non-passers. Stronger effect than MobyMax mastery.
MobyMax: Does mastering grade levels matter? YES, mastery matters (+0.89x). Students who master 1+ grade levels show significantly higher growth (2.95x vs 2.06x). Clear positive correlation with more grades mastered.
eGumpp: Does mastery matter? All eGumpp students perform well (3.35x-6.75x). Small sample and pre-selection bias make it hard to isolate mastery impact. These students already mastered G6 MobyMax before entering eGumpp.
MobyMax-only: Does mastery matter? Mixed results. Students with 0 grades mastered actually show higher growth (2.47x) than 1-2 grades. Suggests mastery speed alone doesn't guarantee growth - engagement and depth may matter more.

Recommendations

  • Prioritize Writing test passage: Writing mastery shows the strongest correlation with MAP growth (+1.33x) - ensure students are progressing through and passing Writing tests
  • Encourage MobyMax mastery: There's a clear +0.89x growth benefit for students who master grade levels
  • Focus on L2 Writing: L2 students show the largest gap (+1.34x) between test-passers and non-passers - prioritize Writing progression for this group
  • Quality over speed: MobyMax-only data suggests rushing through mastery may not translate to MAP growth - focus on deep learning
  • Fix data tracking: The Progress Tracker "Effective Grades Mastered This Year" field is not reflecting passed tests - investigate and correct
  • eGumpp selection is working: Pre-selected students continue to perform well
