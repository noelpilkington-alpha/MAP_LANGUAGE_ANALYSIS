Key Findings

  • Vocabulary: Clear positive association (+1.68x) with nearly all tested students enrolled
  • Writing: Clear positive association (+1.65x) for the 66% of students enrolled
  • Vocab + Writing: This combination shows consistent benefits regardless of Language platform
  • eGumpp Students: Show highest average growth (5.76x), but these are students who have already demonstrated mastery through MobyMax
  • MobyMax Students: Slightly lower averages reflect the broader student population including those still building foundational skills

Interpretation Guidance

  • Do NOT conclude: "eGumpp is better than MobyMax" - the data doesn't support this due to selection bias
  • DO conclude: Writing and Vocabulary enrollment show clear positive associations with MAP growth
  • DO conclude: Students who reach eGumpp (after mastering G6 MobyMax) continue to show strong growth
  • DO conclude: The combination of Vocab + Writing benefits students regardless of their Language platform

Recommended Actions

  • Prioritize Writing and Vocabulary enrollment for all students
  • Continue using MobyMax as the foundation for Language skill-building
  • Track student progression from MobyMax to eGumpp as an indicator of mastery
  • Focus on ensuring students complete the full curriculum pathway rather than comparing platforms
