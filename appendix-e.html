Important: Selection Bias Note

Students are only enrolled in eGumpp after mastering Grade 6 Language in MobyMax. This means eGumpp students are pre-selected for higher performance. Direct comparisons between eGumpp and MobyMax should not be interpreted as platform effectiveness comparisons.

The higher eGumpp average (5.26x) reflects student selection, not platform superiority.

Critical Context: Selection Bias

eGumpp students are NOT randomly assigned. They are enrolled only after mastering Grade 6 Language in MobyMax. This means eGumpp students represent higher-performing students who have already demonstrated proficiency. The +2.12x difference reflects student selection, not platform effectiveness. Both groups have identical "below expected" rates (43%), suggesting similar instructional quality once adjusted for starting point.

Enrollment Combination Insights

  • Writing + Vocabulary together (3.75x) is the most effective widely-used combination (n=159)
  • Adding Writing to MobyMax doesn't significantly change results (3.36x vs 3.14x), but both exceed target
  • Vocabulary alone (2.62x) still meets the 2x target but underperforms combinations
  • Writing alone (1.25x) is below target, but sample size is only n=1
  • eGumpp combinations rank highest but reflect selection bias (pre-selected high performers)

Key Takeaways

  • MobyMax IS working: 3.14x average growth for non-HS students exceeds the 2x target
  • Writing XP goals matter: +0.53x higher growth for students meeting Writing XP goals
  • Combination benefits: Writing + Vocabulary together (3.75x) outperforms either alone
  • Don't compare platforms directly: eGumpp vs MobyMax is confounded by selection bias
